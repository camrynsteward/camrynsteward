Olympic Manifesto

1892 Founding Document

$8.8 million – De Coubertin wrote the 14-page manifesto in a bid to relaunch the Olympic Games, presenting it alongside a speech at the Sorbonne University in Paris.

Just two years later, he was credited with founding the first modern Olympic Games, which were held in Athens in 1896.

The original Olympic Games Manifesto, written in 1892, was sold at a Sotheby’s auction for a record-setting price of $8.8 million to an undisclosed buyer.
